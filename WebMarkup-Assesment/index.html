3 Technologies that are changing Sports

Artificial Intelligence (AI)

AI is essentially technology that mimics human senses, thoughts, and behavioral abilities, and AI-based systems are already in use in virtually every major sports discipline. Sensors, wearables, and computer vision-based cameras all use AI to process data and provide predictive models. At the same time, natural language processing devices can use speech and text recognition to gain insight into the audience's emotions.

Influencer Marketing

As digitization continues to evolve, brands are increasingly focusing on partnering with influencers, suggesting this trend is likely to gain even more momentum in 2022. TikTok is the current social platform. and the fastest growing social media network of all time. Instagram has introduced a collaboration feature where posts appear on both users' accounts at the same time, encouraging engagement and reaching a wider audience. These trends and platforms are innovative and effective ways to generate revenue and increase awareness, so anyone looking to grow their marketing career in esports should keep a close eye on them.

E Sports & Fan Experience

The esports industry, currently valued at $1.8 billion in revenue, is expected to surpass $2.5 billion next year, making it one of the few sports entertainment companies to thrive financially amid the global pandemic.
